## 1. Legal Guidelines for Medical Treatment
This part is formulated in accordance with national laws and regulations such as the Mental Health Law of the People's Republic of China and the Civil Code of the People's Republic of China, clarifying the statutory obligations and rights of medical institutions, medical staff, adolescents and their guardians.

### 1.1 Statutory Cooperation Principles for Medical Treatment
- **Exclusive professional diagnosis**: Diagnosis and treatment of depressive disorder can only be completed by qualified mental health physicians and medical institutions. Self-assessment results via scales do not equal clinical diagnosis, and medical institutions are prohibited from entrusting diagnosis and treatment to unqualified personnel.
- **Compliance with treatment plans**: Without the evaluation of the attending physician, the patient shall not arbitrarily stop medication, reduce the dosage, terminate treatment or change the treatment plan.

### 1.2 Privacy and Personal Information Protection
- Medical institutions and staff bear the statutory confidentiality obligation for the patient's condition information, diagnosis and treatment records, personal identity information, psychological assessment results and other privacy. Disclosure to third parties is not allowed without statutory circumstances or written consent.
- **Statutory exception**: When the patient has an emergency risk of self-harm, suicide or endangering others' personal safety, medical institutions can disclose necessary information to guardians and relevant emergency departments only to eliminate the emergency risk.
- Patients and guardians have the right to claim civil compensation and file complaints if they find illegal disclosure of privacy by medical institutions or staff.

### 1.3 Statutory Rights of Patients and Guardians
- **Informed consent right**: Physicians shall fully inform the patient and guardians of diagnosis results, treatment plans, drug adverse reactions, medical risks, cost details and prognosis in an easy-to-understand way; major treatment decisions require the guardian to sign an informed consent form.
- **Treatment plan negotiation and referral right**: The patient and guardians have the right to communicate and negotiate with physicians on the treatment plan; if the negotiation is inconsistent, they can apply for referral independently, and the medical institution shall cooperate to provide medical records and other materials.
- **Medical record access and reproduction right**: The right to check and copy all diagnosis and treatment materials such as outpatient medical records, scale assessment reports and prescriptions, and the medical institution only charges the cost of reproduction.
- **Cost supervision and verification right**: The right to check the detailed cost of all medical items and require the medical institution to issue official financial bills; complaints can be filed for irregular charging behaviors.

### 1.4 Special Requirements for Adolescent Medical Treatment
Adolescents aged 8-17 must be accompanied by guardians (parents or legal agents) throughout the treatment process. All civil acts such as statutory informed consent, fee settlement, medical record inspection and referral application shall be exercised by guardians on their behalf.

### 1.5 Complaint Channels
- Submit a written complaint to the medical affairs department/complaint office of the attending hospital, which shall reply within 7 working days.
- File a complaint with the local health commission and market supervision and administration bureau.
- Reflect the problem through the 12345 government service hotline.

### 1.6 Medical Record Retention
Outpatient medical records shall be kept by medical institutions for at least 15 years without arbitrary damage or loss; patients and guardians shall properly keep registration forms, fee bills, medical records and other diagnosis and treatment materials as the legal basis for follow-up visits, referral and medical insurance reimbursement.

## 2. Clinical Diagnosis Criteria (ICD-11)
The diagnosis of adolescent depressive disorder is based on the 11th Revision of the International Classification of Diseases (ICD-11, code 6A70-6A7Z), with the core of comprehensive evaluation of symptom clustering and functional impairment.

### 2.1 Core Diagnostic Features
For at least two weeks, nearly every day and for most of the day, there is either depressed mood (sadness, irritability, emptiness) or loss of interest/pleasure in daily activities (one of the two is a necessary condition for diagnosis).

### 2.2 Associated Symptoms
At least five symptoms from the following two categories are required for diagnosis:
- Cognitive-behavioral cluster: Difficulty concentrating, low self-esteem, inappropriate guilt, hopelessness, recurrent thoughts of death or suicide.
- Neurovegetative cluster: Sleep or appetite disturbance, decreased energy.

### 2.3 Severity Classification and Functional Impairment
- Classified into mild, moderate and severe according to the intensity of symptoms and the degree of functional impairment; moderate depressive episodes can be marked as "with psychotic symptoms", and partial remission and complete remission statuses are distinguished.
- The symptoms must cause significant impairment in personal, family, social, academic or other important functional areas; if the patient maintains normal function, he or she needs to pay considerable extra effort.

### 2.4 Exclusion Criteria
Individuals with a history of manic, mixed or hypomanic episodes shall be diagnosed with bipolar disorder rather than depressive disorder.

## 3. Evidence-Based Intervention Approaches
The intervention plan for adolescent depressive disorder is formulated individually based on the severity of the condition, individual differences and evidence-based evidence, following the principles of safety, standardization and efficiency. Professional assessment by qualified mental health professionals is the fundamental prerequisite for all interventions.

### 3.1 Pharmacotherapy
An important intervention method for moderate to severe adolescent depressive disorder, which needs to be used under the comprehensive evaluation of professional staff, and the efficacy and safety shall be continuously monitored during the treatment period. Common antidepressants are divided into 6 categories:
- Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs): Sertraline, Citalopram, Escitalopram, etc.
- Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs): Venlafaxine, Duloxetine, etc.
- Noradrenergic and Specific Serotonergic Antidepressants (NaSSAs): Mirtazapine, etc.
- Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs): Amitriptyline, Imipramine, Clomipramine, Doxepin, etc.
- Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOIs): Moclobemide, Phenelzine, etc.
- Other mechanism-based antidepressants: Bupropion, Trazodone, Mianserin, etc.
- For patients with poor effect of single drug treatment, combined medication can be carried out under the guidance of physicians, with strict avoidance of drug interactions.

### 3.2 Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
The first-line psychological intervention program for mild to moderate adolescent depressive disorder, with core technologies including cognitive restructuring, behavioral activation, exposure therapy, problem-solving training, mindfulness stress reduction training, etc., to improve patients' negative cognitive patterns and interpersonal interaction functions.

### 3.3 Psychological Counseling
Can be used alone for mild depressive patients, or as an auxiliary means for drug therapy and psychological therapy of moderate to severe patients. Common methods include supportive psychological counseling, Interpersonal Therapy (IPT), Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT), psychodynamic therapy, group psychological counseling, etc.

### 3.4 Physical Therapy
Applicable to moderate to severe depressive disorder patients or patients with poor response to drug and psychological therapy, which shall be carried out in professional medical institutions. Common methods include repetitive Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (rTMS), Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT), Vagus Nerve Stimulation (VNS), Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS), etc.

### 3.5 Adjuvant Therapy
As a supplementary means of comprehensive intervention, it is implemented in coordination with the core treatment plan, including regular exercise therapy, mindfulness meditation training, art therapy, music therapy, social support intervention, etc.

### 3.6 Combined Therapy
For patients with severe symptoms, suicidal risk or combined with other psychological and behavioral problems, the combination of psychological therapy and pharmacotherapy is recommended to improve the overall intervention effect and the stability of prognosis.

### 3.7 Controversy on Pharmacotherapy and Suicide Risk
The correlation between antidepressant treatment and suicide risk in adolescents is still controversial. Some early studies suggest that the risk of suicidal ideation or behavior may increase slightly in the early stage of drug treatment, but the overall absolute risk is low, and the risk may gradually decrease with the extension of treatment time. Therefore, strict risk monitoring is required in the early stage of medication and dosage adjustment period.

- Physical therapy requires strict evaluation of indications and is only performed by professional doctors for moderate to severe cases or patients with poor drug treatment effect.
- Adjuvant therapy cannot replace the core treatment plan and is only a supplementary means to improve mood and promote recovery.

## 7. Evidence Consensus and Research Gaps
### 7.1 Evidence Consensus
- Adolescent depressive disorder has a high prevalence and causes significant disease burden to individuals, families and society, which has become an urgent public health issue.
- Early identification, professional assessment and evidence-based intervention can effectively improve the prognosis of adolescent depressive disorder patients, reduce suicide risk and improve their quality of life.
- Suicide risk is closely related to depressive disorder, so it is necessary to carry out continuous monitoring and systematic management of suicide risk for depressive disorder patients and strengthen risk prevention and control.

### 7.2 Research Gaps
- The long-term effect differences of different intervention strategies in adolescents with different subtypes of depressive disorder are unclear, and there is a lack of targeted intervention guidelines.
- The causal relationship between antidepressant treatment and adolescent suicide risk and related moderating factors (age, gender, disease severity) have not been fully clarified.
- How to realize the sustainable integration of screening, treatment and follow-up of adolescent depressive disorder under different cultural backgrounds and resource allocation conditions, and improve the accessibility of intervention, still need further exploration.
